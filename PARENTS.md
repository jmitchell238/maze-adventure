# Maze Adventure — notes for parents

Built for kids about **4 and 6**, with harder modes for older siblings and adults.

## Modes at a glance

| Mode | Best for |
|------|----------|
| **Adventure** | First play — short stages, unlocks cute worlds |
| **Quick maze** | Pick Easy → Very Hard anytime |
| **Daily maze** | One maze per day (no internet needed) |
| **Free play** | You choose size, help level, and theme |

## Tips for ages 4–6

1. Start with **Adventure** or **Quick → Easy**.  
2. Settings → **Movement: Guided** (or leave Auto).  
3. **Tap the next square** to move — feels natural for little hands. Drag to use the stick, or use arrows/WASD on a computer.  
4. Tap **?** for a sparkle trail — unlimited on Easy, never shames the player.  
5. If they sit in a dead end, a soft helper appears after a couple of seconds.  
6. No lives, no fail screens, no ads, no timers on Easy.

## Optional settings

- **Reduced motion** — calmer animation  
- **High contrast path** — stronger path/wall colors  
- **Spoken praise** — cheerful voice on win (uses the device speech feature)  
- **Show maze seed** — for sharing a specific maze (debug / curiosity)

## Hard mode gates

On Hard / Very Hard (and Free play with “Switch + gate”), a **yellow switch** opens a **locked gate**. Hints point to the switch first.

## Progress

Saved only on this device (`localStorage`). Clearing browser data resets Adventure unlocks.

## Privacy

- No accounts  
- No tracking  
- No network required after the page is cached as a PWA
